Music App Experience in the Art Gallery of NSW

DECO2014 User Experience Studio Project

Overview

Art galleries and museums are educational and inspirational institutions which receive many visitors hoping for a unique experience. For the unit DECO2014 User Experience, I collaborated with my group partner to explore the perceptions students had of the Art Gallery of NSW. From our research, we focused on the aspect of sound during the student journey to develop a new experience that would increase happiness and personalise the gallery experience through sound.

User Research

During the project we incorporated a range of techniques focusing on emphatic design that was learnt in class. This included:

  • Gallery walkthroughs
  • Generative sessions - including the use of sensitising booklets, interactive exercises and interviews
  • Affinity diagramming

The goal of our research was to learn about the emotions visitors went through in their gallery journey. We created sensitising booklets to get our participants thinking about what makes a good experience before their generative session where we focused on their Art Gallery journey.

Concept Creation

The final design is of a digital wayfinding station with the following features:

  • Curved shelter for rainy weather and privacy
  • Digital touchscreen for convenience
  • Simple, user-friendly interface
  • Separate keyboard for ergonomic comfort
  • Voice-enabled direction for access and understanding
  • QR code for portability
